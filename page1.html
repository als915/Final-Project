Why is it called the Bridge of Sighs? The Bridge of Sighs Venice was built at
the beginning of the Seventeenth Century
and spanned the Rio di Palazzo. Those who crossed the Bridge of Sighs
were likely going to be incarcerated
for a very long time. So, the theory is that those who traversed
the bridge sighed as they looked out
and saw their last sight of
freedom. They say this is why it is called
the Bridge of Sighs.
The poet Lord Byron also
popularized this theory in some of his
writing.
